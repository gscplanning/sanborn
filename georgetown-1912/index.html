Georgetown 1912 Sanborn Map

A responsive sidebar for mapping libraries like Leaflet or OpenLayers.

It's easy to take for granted nowadays the ease with which one can obtain information about a place. Platforms like GoogleMaps, ArcGIS Online, and OpenStreetMap as well as derivative applications like Yelp!, Zillow, and our own interactive maps here at the Planning Commission allow users to have data-drenched experiences that help themunderstand and navigate the hyperlocal scale of their world.

Sanborn created maps so that insurance companies could determine if your building would be insured and how much it would cost you.
